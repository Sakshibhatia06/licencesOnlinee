GST Revocation Overview

If your GST registration was cancelled due to missed filings, non-compliance, or procedural defaults, you may still be eligible to restore it by applying for GST Revocation. The process can seem complex—requiring documentation, justification, and timely response to notices—but it is essential to resume legal business operations.

Revocation is submitted through Form GST REG-21 on the GST portal and must be completed within 30 days (or extended period, if allowed) from the date of the cancellation order. Timely action ensures uninterrupted tax compliance, reinstates your GSTIN, and helps avoid penalties or legal liabilities.

What is GST Revocation?

GST Revocation is the process of legally reactivating a cancelled GST registration. If the cancellation was initiated by the tax authorities (not voluntarily), businesses can appeal for revocation. Once approved, the registration is restored, allowing the business to continue issuing tax invoices, filing returns, and claiming input tax credit.

Why is GST Revocation Important?

  • Avoid Business Disruption: Without an active GSTIN, you cannot operate legally under GST laws.
  • Resume Filing Returns: Filing returns becomes possible only after revocation is approved.
  • Restore Input Tax Credit: You can claim ITC again only after GSTIN is active.
  • Issue Legal Invoices: Reactivation enables you to generate valid GST invoices and e-way bills.

Why is GST Registration Revoked?

  • Non-Filing of GST Returns: Repeated failure to file returns leads to automatic cancellation.
  • Business Closure: If operations have ceased permanently, registration may be revoked.
  • Inactivity: Long periods without taxable supplies may cause cancellation.
  • Non-Compliance: Engaging in tax evasion, fraud, or violating GST rules triggers revocation.
  • Change in Structure: Mergers, transfers, or restructuring may require registration revocation.
  • Voluntary Cancellation: If turnover falls below the threshold, businesses can request cancellation—but revocation applies only if the cancellation was initiated by authorities.

Steps Involved in the Revocation Process:

  • Login to GST portal and go to ‘Application for Revocation of Cancellation’
  • Submit Form GST REG-21 with the reason for revocation and supporting documents
  • Tax officer may respond with REG-23 for clarification
  • Reply with REG-24 and any further documentation
  • Receive approval order in Form REG-22 upon successful verification

Key Points to Remember:

  • Revocation request must be filed within 30 days (or as extended)
  • Clear all pending tax liabilities and file overdue returns before applying
  • Only departmental cancellations are eligible for revocation, not voluntary exits

Detailed Process:

  1. Application for Revocation: Begin by logging into the GST portal and navigating to the 'Application for Revocation of Cancellation of Registration' tab. Fill in Form GST REG-21 and upload the necessary supporting documents. In rare instances, offline filing may be permitted by submitting physical documents to the jurisdictional officer.
  2. Scrutiny by Tax Officer: Once the application is submitted, the tax officer will evaluate the reasons for revocation and verify whether all GST compliance obligations have been met. If additional details are required, a clarification notice (Form REG-23) may be issued, which must be addressed through Form REG-24.
  3. Final Order: After assessment, the GST officer will either approve the application and issue Form REG-22 (restoring your GST registration), or reject it, providing reasons for the denial. Rejected applicants can reapply after resolving the highlighted issues.

GST Revocation Time Frame & Process Duration

The time taken to complete the GST revocation process may vary based on the accuracy of your application, supporting documentation, and the response time of the assigned GST officer. On average, the full process spans 7 to 14 working days from submission.

1. Application Submission

Form GST REG-21 is submitted online through the GST portal. This can take from a few minutes up to 2 working days depending on document readiness and verification.

2. Officer Scrutiny

The jurisdictional GST officer reviews the application and checks compliance status. This typically takes 5 to 10 working days. Officers may issue a clarification notice (REG-23) if needed.

3. Final Decision

Post-verification, the officer either approves the revocation by issuing REG-22 or rejects it with reasons. This decision is generally made within 1 to 3 working days.

Consequences of GST Revocation
Failing to properly manage GST registration revocation can significantly impact business operations. Below are the key consequences if your GST registration is cancelled and not revoked in time:
Reversal of Input Tax Credit (ITC): You may be required to reverse all previously claimed ITC, leading to additional financial liability and reduced working capital.
Disruption in Business Operations: Without an active GSTIN, you cannot issue tax invoices or legally collect GST—this can halt your ability to do business with registered vendors or clients.
Customer Trust Issues: Operating without valid GST registration may reduce customer and vendor confidence, especially in B2B transactions.
Loss of Legal Benefits: Ineligibility for input tax credit, government tenders, or subsidies until registration is restored.
Compliance Penalties: Continued non-compliance or failure to respond to revocation notices can attract heavy fines, penalties, or even prosecution under the GST Act.
GST Revocation FAQs

What is GST revocation?

GST revocation is the process of restoring a GST registration that was previously cancelled by the tax authorities. It allows a business to resume GST-related activities legally.

When can I apply for revocation of GST cancellation?

You can apply for revocation within 30 days (or extended period as allowed) from the date of service of the cancellation order, provided the cancellation was not due to voluntary closure.

What are the consequences of not revoking a cancelled GST registration?

If not revoked, you cannot issue tax invoices or collect GST legally. Input tax credit (ITC) must be reversed, and your business may face compliance issues, penalties, or loss of credibility.

Do I need to file pending returns before applying for revocation?

Yes, you must clear all pending GST returns, pay dues, and resolve compliance issues before the revocation application is accepted.

What happens after revocation is approved?

Your GSTIN becomes active again, allowing you to continue regular filings, issue invoices, and claim input tax credit (ITC). You must stay compliant to avoid future cancellations.

Can I claim ITC after revocation of GST registration?

Once registration is reinstated, you may continue to claim eligible ITC on business purchases, provided all compliance obligations are fulfilled.

Is revocation applicable if I voluntarily cancelled my GST?

No, revocation is only applicable if the GST registration was cancelled by the department. Voluntary cancellations cannot be revoked—you need to apply for fresh registration.

How do I apply for GST revocation?

Login to the GST portal, go to "Services > Registration > Application for Revocation of Cancelled Registration", submit reasons with supporting documents, and file the request digitally.

Can revocation be rejected by the GST officer?

Yes, if the officer finds the reason or documents unsatisfactory, the application may be rejected. However, a show-cause notice is issued before rejection to allow a response.

What are the risks if I continue business without revoking GST?

Continuing operations without a valid GSTIN is illegal. It can lead to fines, denial of ITC, seizure of goods, and even prosecution under the GST Act.
