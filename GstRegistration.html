What is GST (Goods and Services Tax)?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is a comprehensive indirect tax system implemented in India on July 1, 2017, replacing multiple cascading taxes imposed by the central and state governments.

It is a single tax levied on the supply of goods and services across all stages of the supply chain, from manufacturer to consumer. GST aims to create a unified, transparent, and efficient tax regime.

Key Features of GST:

  • One Nation, One Tax ‚Äì Unified tax system across India
  • Multi-stage, destination-based tax
  • Applicable to goods, services, imports, and exports
  • Types:
    • CGST ‚Äì Central GST
    • SGST ‚Äì State GST
    • IGST ‚Äì Integrated GST (for inter-state supply)

What is GST Registration?

GST Registration is the official process of enrolling your business under the GST law. Upon successful registration, a business is issued a GSTIN (Goods and Services Tax Identification Number).

Who Needs GST Registration?

  • Businesses with turnover above ‚Çπ40 lakhs (goods) / ‚Çπ20 lakhs (services)
  • Inter-state suppliers of goods/services
  • eCommerce sellers and marketplace vendors
  • Casual taxable persons or non-resident suppliers
  • TDS deductors and input service distributors (ISDs)

Why GST Registration is Important:

  • Legalizes your business under GST law
  • Allows collection of GST from customers
  • Enables Input Tax Credit (ITC) claims
  • Mandatory for online sellers and inter-state suppliers
  • Boosts business credibility and transparency

After Registration:

  • Receive a 15-digit GSTIN
  • File monthly, quarterly, or annual GST returns
  • Download your GST certificate from the portal
  • Comply with invoicing and record-keeping regulations

Types of GST Registration

The type of GST registration depends on the nature and size of your business. Based on your supply pattern, turnover, or business presence, you can choose the appropriate GST registration category listed below.

1. Regular Taxpayer

Default option for businesses that exceed the threshold limit. Must file monthly/quarterly returns and maintain full compliance.

2. Composition Scheme

For small businesses with turnover up to ‚Çπ1.5 crore. Lower tax rates, reduced compliance, but no input tax credit allowed.

3. Casual Taxable Person

For businesses operating temporarily in another state (e.g., at trade shows or exhibitions). Requires advance tax payment.

4. Non-Resident Taxable Person

For individuals or businesses based outside India but supplying goods/services within India. Registration is mandatory.

5. E-Commerce Operator

Compulsory for those running eCommerce platforms or aggregators. Includes marketplaces like Amazon, Flipkart, etc.

6. TDS/TCS Deductor

Government departments or eCommerce platforms deducting tax at source must register under this category.

What is GSTIN?
GSTIN or Goods and Services Tax Identification Number is a unique 15-digit alphanumeric code provided by the Goods and Services Tax Department to every registered taxpayer under GST. It is based on the taxpayer‚Äôs PAN and state code, and serves as an identity number for businesses registered under the GST system.
GSTIN is used by authorities to track compliance and filings, and by businesses to validate other suppliers and vendors. It ensures transparency in the taxation system and allows businesses to claim Input Tax Credit on their purchases. Every invoice, return, and communication with the GST department must quote this number.
Structure: 15 digits comprising state code, PAN, entity code, and checksum digit
Issued: Upon successful GST registration through the official GST portal
Mandatory: For businesses with turnover above threshold limits and certain specified categories
Used For: Filing GST returns, generating e-invoices, and availing tax credits
Publicly Verifiable: Anyone can verify a GSTIN on the official GST portal
GST Registration Certificate
A GST Registration Certificate is an official document issued by the GST department upon successful registration. This certificate acts as legal proof of registration and contains important details about the taxpayer, including the GSTIN, business name, address, and date of registration.
Downloadable: Can be downloaded from the GST portal once registration is approved
Mandatory Display: Must be prominently displayed at the primary place of business
Includes: Legal name, trade name, GSTIN, business address, and registration type
Verification: QR code provided for authenticity verification
Update: Certificate gets updated upon any change in registration details

GST Registration FAQs

What is GST registration?

GST registration is a process by which a taxpayer gets registered under Goods and Services Tax. Once registered, a unique GSTIN is assigned. It enables businesses to collect and remit GST and claim input tax credit.

Is GST registration mandatory?

Yes, it is mandatory for businesses whose turnover exceeds prescribed threshold limits, or those involved in inter-state supply, e-commerce, or specified services irrespective of turnover.

How to get GST number?

You can apply for a GST number online on the official GST portal by submitting the required documents and completing verification.

What is the GST registration number?

The GST registration number (GSTIN) is a 15-digit alphanumeric code assigned after successful registration, used for all GST compliance and transactions.

What is the threshold limit for GST registration?

‚Çπ40 lakhs for goods in normal states, ‚Çπ20 lakhs for services; lower limits apply to special category states. Exceeding this requires mandatory registration.

What documents are required for GST registration?

Documents such as PAN card, Aadhaar card, business address proof, bank account details, and photographs of the proprietor/partners/directors are typically required.

Can I apply for GST registration without a business address?

Yes, you can apply using a residential address or co-working space. Proper supporting documents are needed for verification.

How long does it take to get a GST number?

The GST number is usually issued within 3-7 working days after submission, depending on document accuracy and verification timelines.

Can I cancel my GST registration?

Yes, if you close your business or your turnover falls below the threshold, you can apply for cancellation on the GST portal.

Is there a penalty for not registering under GST?

Yes, a penalty of ‚Çπ10,000 or 10% of the tax due (whichever is higher) may be imposed for non-registration.
