GST Filing Overview

Goods and Services Tax (GST) Filing is the process by which a registered taxpayer submits returns to the government containing details of their business activities such as sales, purchases, output GST collected, and input tax credit (ITC) claimed.

Filing GST returns accurately and on time is essential for businesses to remain compliant, avoid penalties, and maintain credibility.

Why Is GST Filing Important?

  • for all registered businesses: Whether your business is involved in trading, manufacturing, eCommerce, or services, if you’re registered under GST, filing returns is a legal obligation.
  • Helps claim Input Tax Credit (ITC): Filing returns properly enables businesses to claim credits for the tax paid on purchases, which reduces their overall tax liability.
  • Avoid penalties and interest: Late or incorrect filings attract hefty penalties and interest, impacting business finances.
  • Builds trust and transparency: A timely GST compliance record enhances your company’s reliability in the eyes of suppliers, partners, and clients.

Who Needs to File GST Returns?

  • Regular taxpayers (monthly/quarterly filings like GSTR-1, GSTR-3B)
  • Composition scheme holders (GSTR-4 annually)
  • Non-resident taxpayers (GSTR-5)
  • Input Service Distributors (GSTR-6)
  • E-commerce operators (GSTR-8)
  • TDS/TCS deductors (GSTR-7)
  • Businesses undergoing cancellation (GSTR-10)
  • UIN holders (GSTR-11 for refund claims)

Types of Returns Filed:

Each type of return serves a unique purpose:

  • GSTR-1: Details of outward supplies (sales)
  • GSTR-3B: Summary of all GST liabilities and ITC
  • GSTR-9: Annual return
  • GSTR-4: For composition taxpayers

How Often Are Returns Filed?

  • Monthly: GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B for regular taxpayers
  • Quarterly: For small taxpayers under QRMP scheme
  • Annually: GSTR-4 (Composition) and GSTR-9 (Annual Return)
  • Comply with invoicing and record-keeping regulations

Types of GST Filings in India

Under the Goods and Services Tax (GST) regime in India, different types of returns are prescribed for various categories of taxpayers to ensure accurate and timely reporting of business transactions. Each GST return has a unique purpose and filing frequency based on the taxpayer’s registration type and activities.

1. GSTR-1

is a return for outward supplies, where regular taxpayers report details of their sales and invoices. It is filed monthly or quarterly (under the QRMP scheme) and forms the basis for recipients to claim Input Tax Credit. GSTR-3B is a monthly summary return that includes the total sales, purchases, tax liability, and available ITC.

2. GSTR-5

is applicable to non-resident foreign taxpayers who conduct business in India without a fixed place of operation. This return captures all their inward and outward supplies and is filed monthly. GSTR-6 is meant for Input Service Distributors (ISDs), allowing them to report and distribute input tax credit to their units or branches.

3. GSTR-7

is required to be filed by entities who are liable to deduct Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) under GST, such as government departments or agencies. On the other hand, GSTR-8 is filed by e-commerce operators who are obligated to collect Tax Collected at Source (TCS) on behalf of the sellers using their platform.

4. GSTR-9

is an annual return to be filed by regular taxpayers, which consolidates all the monthly or quarterly returns filed during the financial year. Businesses with an annual turnover of more than ₹5 crore are also required to file GSTR-9C, a reconciliation statement certified by a Chartered Accountant.

5. GSTR-11

is filed by UIN holders, such as diplomatic missions or international organizations, for claiming refunds on GST paid on their inward supplies. Each type of GST return plays a vital role in the smooth functioning and transparency of the GST system.

GST Filing Due Dates and Deadlines – 2025
Timely filing of GST returns is crucial to avoid penalties, interest, and disruptions in Input Tax Credit (ITC). Here's a structured overview of monthly and quarterly GST filing schedules for the year 2025:
GSTR-1
Monthly: Due by 11th of the following month for businesses with turnover above ₹5 crores.
Quarterly: Due by 13th of the month following the quarter for QRMP scheme taxpayers (turnover up to ₹5 crores).
IFF (Invoice Furnishing Facility)
Optional for QRMP taxpayers to report invoices monthly. Due by 13th of the following month (for first two months of the quarter).
GSTR-3B
Monthly: Due by 20th of the following month for businesses with turnover above ₹5 crores.
Quarterly:
  • 22nd of the month following the quarter for Category A states
  • 24th for Category B states
CMP-08
For composition taxpayers to declare and pay tax quarterly. Due by 18th of the month following the quarter.

GST Refund Process & Reconciliation

Businesses may be eligible for GST refunds in cases of excess tax payment, export transactions, or unutilized input credit. Efficient reconciliation ensures smooth processing and prevents compliance issues.

1. Identify Refund Type

Check eligibility for refunds due to export without tax, excess tax paid, input tax credit (ITC) accumulation, or tax on cancelled transactions.

2. Collect Supporting Documents

Prepare tax invoices, shipping bills (for exports), GST RFD-01 form, payment proofs, and account details for submission.

3. File Refund Application

Log in to the GST portal and apply under the appropriate refund category using Form GST RFD-01 along with required documents.

4. Track & Receive Refund

After tax officer verification, the approved refund is credited to your bank account within 60 days. Any delay incurs applicable interest.

GST Reconciliation Importance

  • Monthly Matching: Compare supplier data (GSTR-2B) with your purchase records to ensure correct ITC claims.
  • Outward vs Return Data: Reconcile GSTR-1 with GSTR-3B to avoid discrepancies in sales reporting and tax liability.
  • Avoid Notice & Penalties: Mismatched data can trigger scrutiny or ITC denial. Regular checks reduce such risks.
  • Use Automation Tools: Integrate GST reconciliation tools with accounting software for faster and error-free compliance.
GST Filing Penalties
Not filing your GST returns on time or providing incorrect details can attract penalties and interest. Here are the key penalties associated with non-compliance in GST return filing:
Late Filing of GSTR-3B: ₹50/day of delay (₹20 for NIL return), up to ₹5,000 per return
Interest on Late Payment: 18% per annum on unpaid tax from the due date till the actual payment date
Wrong Information Filing: ₹25,000 penalty for filing incorrect returns or mismatched data
Non-Filing Consequences: Continuous failure to file returns may result in suspension or cancellation of GSTIN
Wrong ITC Claim: 100% penalty on wrong input tax credit claimed fraudulently
GST Filing FAQs

What is GST filing?

GST filing is the process of submitting business sales, purchases, and tax collected to the government through returns such as GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, and GSTR-9.

Who is required to file GST returns?

Any registered GST taxpayer (individual, business, or company) must file returns as per their category—monthly, quarterly, or annually—even if there is no transaction.

What happens if I don’t file GST returns?

Late filing attracts a penalty and interest. Continuous failure may lead to GSTIN suspension or cancellation and ineligibility for Input Tax Credit (ITC).

Which GST returns need to be filed regularly?

Most businesses file GSTR-1 (outward supplies) monthly or quarterly and GSTR-3B (summary return) monthly. Annual GSTR-9 is required for businesses over a turnover threshold.

Can I file GST returns myself?

Yes, if you’re comfortable with portal navigation, invoice matching, and compliance. However, most businesses prefer CA assistance to avoid errors and penalties.

What is the due date for GST return filing?

GSTR-3B is usually due by the 20th of the following month, and GSTR-1 by the 11th. QRMP scheme filers have staggered due dates. It’s important to check the GST calendar each month.

Is GST filing required if there is no sale?

Yes. Even if there are zero transactions, NIL returns must be filed to stay compliant and avoid penalties.

How long does it take to file GST returns?

If all invoices and ITC data are ready, GST returns can be filed within a few hours. With professional help, the process is quicker and error-free.

Can I revise a filed GST return?

You cannot revise a GST return directly. However, corrections can be made in subsequent returns by adjusting the figures or adding the missed invoices.

Is professional help mandatory for GST filing?

No, it’s not mandatory. But it’s highly recommended for accuracy, timely compliance, and avoiding penalties especially for growing businesses.
